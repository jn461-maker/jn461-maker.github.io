About Deborah

Hello, I'm Deborah.

I am a qualified Integrative Counsellor (MBACP) working in Cambridgeshire and online. "Integrative" simply means I don't believe in a one-size-fits-all approach to therapy.

I combine different therapeutic methods - drawing from psychodynamic insights, person-centred empathy, and practical tools - to suit you. Sometimes we might explore your past to understand present patterns; other times, we focus on strategies to get you through the week.

Having worked in both community services and the corporate world, I understand the unique pressures of modern life. I offer a down-to-earth, non-judgemental partnership where you can be your messy, unpolished self.

Areas of Focus

How I Can Help

While I work with the whole person, not just a list of symptoms, these are common areas where I offer specialised support.

Anxiety & Stress

When worry feels constant or physical symptoms (racing heart, sleeplessness) take over. We'll work to understand the root causes and build a toolkit for regulation.

Panic Attacks Burnout GAD

Loss & Bereavement

Grief doesn't follow a timeline. Whether it's the death of a loved one, baby loss, or the end of a relationship, I offer a patient space to process the pain.

Grief Miscarriage Life Changes

Identity & Self-Esteem

Feeling like you don't fit in, or struggling with your sense of self? We can explore perfectionism, confidence, and the "masks" we wear to survive.

Confidence Identity Imposter Syndrome

Relationship Issues

Navigating conflict, betrayal, or recurring patterns in relationships. I help you understand your needs and boundaries, whether you attend individually or for perspective.

Family Divorce Betrayal

Trauma & Safety

Working gently with past experiences that shape your present. We move at a pace that ensures you feel safe and in control, never pushing before you are ready.

PTSD Abuse Childhood Trauma

Neurodiversity

Support for living with ADHD, Autism, or chronic conditions. I offer a neuro-affirming space to discuss the unique challenges and strengths you possess.

ADHD Autism Chronic Illness

How Counselling Works

Starting can be the hardest part. Here is a transparent look at the process.

Reach Out

Send a message via the form below or email me. I aim to respond within 24 hours to arrange a brief, free initial chat or book a first session.

The First Session

A 50-minute space to tell your story. I'll ask about what brought you here, and you can ask me how I work. There is no pressure to commit immediately.

Ongoing Support

We meet weekly or fortnightly. You are in the driving seat - we work on what feels important to you that week, reviewing our progress regularly.
